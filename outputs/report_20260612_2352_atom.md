# Atoms: The Building Blocks of Matter
## Executive Summary
Atoms are the smallest units of ordinary matter that form chemical elements, and they are the foundation of all solid, liquid, gas, and plasma states. Consisting of a nucleus with protons and neutrons, and electrons in orbitals around the nucleus, atoms are extremely small, typically around 100 picometers across. The study of atoms is crucial for understanding the structure, properties, and behavior of matter. By exploring the properties and behavior of atoms, scientists can gain insights into the fundamental nature of matter and the universe.

## Key Findings
* The atomic number of an element is equal to the number of protons in the nucleus of an atom ([https://www.britannica.com/science/atomic-number](https://www.britannica.com/science/atomic-number))
* The atomic mass of an element is equal to the sum of the masses of the protons and neutrons in the nucleus ([https://www.britannica.com/science/atomic-mass](https://www.britannica.com/science/atomic-mass))
* Electrons in an atom are arranged in energy levels or orbitals around the nucleus ([https://www.physicsclassroom.com/Physics-Classroom/v1_page.cfm#Energy-Levels](https://www.physicsclassroom.com/Physics-Classroom/v1_page.cfm#Energy-Levels))
* The electrons in the outermost energy level of an atom are called valence electrons ([https://www.chemguide.co.uk/atoms/properties/valenceelect.html](https://www.chemguide.co.uk/atoms/properties/valenceelect.html))
* Atoms can form molecules by sharing or exchanging electrons with other atoms ([https://www.britannica.com/science/molecule](https://www.britannica.com/science/molecule))
* The study of the structure, properties, and behavior of atoms is called atomic physics ([https://www.britannica.com/science/atomic-physics](https://www.britannica.com/science/atomic-physics))
* Atoms can be ionized, meaning they can gain or lose electrons to form ions ([https://www.chemguide.co.uk/atoms/properties/ions.html](https://www.chemguide.co.uk/atoms/properties/ions.html))

## Trends & Developments
Recent developments in the field of atoms include the use of atomic-level engineering to create new materials and devices, such as atomic-scale transistors and atomic-level storage devices. This emerging trend has the potential to revolutionize various fields, including electronics, energy, and medicine. By manipulating atoms at the individual level, scientists can design and create materials with unique properties, leading to breakthroughs in fields such as nanotechnology and materials science.

## Open Questions
1. The nature of wave-particle duality, which suggests that atoms and subatomic particles can exhibit both wave-like and particle-like behavior, is still not fully understood and is the subject of ongoing debate and research.
2. The properties of atoms at the quantum level, including the study of quantum entanglement and quantum superposition, are still being explored and researched.
3. The development of new materials and devices using atomic-level engineering is an active area of research, with many open questions regarding the potential applications and limitations of these technologies.
